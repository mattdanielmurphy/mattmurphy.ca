Q5: Regression Model Analysis

Does your original data have any outliers?

Not really, the drop is pretty consistent. If I temporarily take out the lowest point (104 in 2022), it barely changes the curve. The overall trend is still a crash.

Which of the two models/equations best describes the actual data?

The logistic model is significantly better. Mathematically, it has a higher R2 value (0.9914 vs 0.9555). Graphically, the logistic curve flattens out slightly at the end, which aligns with the actual data much better than the exponential line, which drops too aggressively early on.

Greatest rate of change? (Using calculus)

  • Exponential: P′(t)=aln(b)⋅bt. Since b<1, the steepest drop is right at the beginning (t=0).
  • Logistic: The max rate of change is at the inflection point, where P′′(t)=0. For this formula, that happens when 1−Aekt=0, so t=k−ln(A)​. Plugging my numbers in: t=0.10558−ln(0.217367)​≈14.45. This means the steepest drop was around the years 1999-2000 (t=14.45). This makes sense because looking at the numbers, the biggest raw drop in the population happened over the two decades between 1995 and 2015.

End behaviour of the models?

  • Exponential limit: As t→∞, a(b)t→0 (because b<1).
  • Logistic limit: As t→∞, the denominator gets infinitely large, so the whole fraction goes to 0.
  • Conclusion: Both equations have a limit of 0. This matches the data perfectly, because if nothing changes, this population is just going to die out.
